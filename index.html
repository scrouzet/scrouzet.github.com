Short bio

My general interest is to understand the perceptual mechanisms underlying visual recognition.

I did a Ph.D. in Neuroscience with Simon Thorpe in Toulouse, France, and a first postdoc with Thomas Serre at Brown University, USA, where I studied the rapid perception of objects in natural scenes using rapid eye movements, iEEG recordings and computational models.

I am now working with Niko Busch in Berlin, where I will use a combination of behavioral and EEG recordings to investigate the role of recurrent processes in visual perception.
