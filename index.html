Sébastien M. Crouzet

Ph.D. in neurosciences
seb.crouzet@gmail.com

My general interest is to understand the perceptual mechanisms underlying visual recognition. I did a Ph.D. in Neuroscience with Simon Thorpe in Toulouse, France, and a first postdoc with Thomas Serre at Brown University, USA, where I studied the rapid perception of objects in natural scenes using eye movements, iEEG recordings and computational models. I am now working with Niko Busch in Berlin, where I will use a combination of behavioral and EEG recordings to investigate the role of recurrent processes in visual perception.

A list of my publications can be found on Mendeley, Google Scholar, PubMed or Frontiers. I'm in the NeuroTree, and try to / will publish code on GitHub.
