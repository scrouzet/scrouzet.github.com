Sébastien M. Crouzet

Ph.D. in neurosciences
seb.crouzet@gmail.com

My general interest is to understand the perceptual mechanisms underlying visual recognition. I did a Ph.D. in Neuroscience with Simon Thorpe in Toulouse, France, and a first postdoc with Thomas Serre at Brown University, USA, where I studied the rapid perception of objects in natural scenes using eye movements, iEEG recordings and computational models. I then moved to Berlin to work with Niko Busch on combining behavioral, eye movements and EEG recordings to investigate the role of recurrent processes and brain oscillations in visual perception. Since September 2014, I joined the team of Rufin VanRullen to study the rythm of visual attention.
